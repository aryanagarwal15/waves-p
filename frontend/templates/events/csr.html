The Ripple Project


In a developing nation like India, our future lies in the hands of the generation we educate today. Education is not- and should not be a luxury. It is a basic necessity. Having said this, there still exists a stark difference in the education and literacy rate between rural and urban areas in India. Though there are many factors that may affect this, lack of resources for education proves to be one of the primary reasons.

Acknowledging this issue and its importance, we wish to play our own part and hopefully make a difference in a few children’s lives. For the second year in a row, Waves will contribute to a cause through its social wing – The Ripple Project. Through comprehensive book collection drives in multiple cities all over the country, we as students plan to give back to society and do our bit.

Drive commences on August 13th and ends September 14th

About our Association:


Ratna Nidhi Charitable Trust, is a registered charity that was established over 24 years ago by Mr Mahendra Mehta. It was set up in order to tackle the problems of poverty in Mumbai, especially amongst young children. From its humble origins as a family institution it has grown rapidly, and now its projects cover a wide range of activities located in both India and other developing countries, including Afghanistan, Sudan, Kenya and Burundi. Ratna Nidhi Charitable Trust is committed to the welfare of people from the most underprivileged strata of society without distinction of caste, creed or color. Its emphasis is on assisting the children and youth who are the citizens of tomorrow and the disabled who need a support to help them join mainstream society. Ratna Nidhi's Mission Million Books project seeks to donate 1 million books to 10,0000 low income schools and colleges in India. Through this project, 7 lakh books have been donated to over 4000 educational institutions in the country. These books are donated to low income schools and colleges. The project seeks to create access to quality reading material for low income educational institutions in India.
